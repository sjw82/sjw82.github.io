Hailey B. Mothershead STEAM Magnet High School

Samantha Watkins' Ideal School Project

The Hailey B. Mothershead STEAM Magnet High School focuses on providing equity in education, meeting students where they are, and providing professional and life skills that students can carry with them well after they leave. Our mission is to prepare students for their lives after graduation, whether they pursue higher education or a career while encouraging strong ties to the community and civic-mindedness. Our philosophy is to entrust students with their education.

H.B.M is located in Homewood, Pittsburgh, PA. By offering busing to districts throughout the city and surrounding areas the school attracts a diverse community of students, though students for whom this school would be zoned as their local school are given priority. Admissions are conducted through a lottery system; the student population is on average 1,400.

There are several educational issues that the school attempts to address across classes: socio-political inequality, access to resources, and life skills. Technology is integrated where possible but never assuming a student has access at home. It is important to us that students have the necessary skills to excel at college or in industry so proficiencies with word, powerpoint, and excel as well as the ability to search for and digest information, for example tutorials, are stressed in most classes. Tutoring is also offered before and after school both teacher to student and peer to peer. There is a second round of buses in the evening to ensure that students whose parents are unable to pick them up late are still able to participate in extracurriculars and additional tutoring. Specific skills such as critical thinking, empathy, problem solving, and discipline are developed not just through material but through class structure. Students are given plenty of opportunity for self expression and to pursue their individual curiosities but are held to a high standard in regards to form and effort.
