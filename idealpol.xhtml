Hailey B. Mothershead STEAM Magnet High School

Policies

There are several educational issues that the school attempts to address across classes: socio-political inequality, access to resources, and life skills. Technology is integrated where possible but never assuming a student has access at home. Tutoring is also offered before and after school; both teacher to student and peer to peer. There is a second round of buses in the evening to ensure that students whose parents are unable to pick them up later in the day are still able to participate in extracurriculars and additional tutoring.

The schools architecture has built in features to address bullying, such as a lack of isolated areas, as well as safety features in case of an intruder, including bullet-resistant glass and designated shelters.

H.B.M. aims to support all students and families, regardless of external circumstances. Through work with community partners, we are able to offer vouchers for daycare and connect students and guardians with services such as job placements, resources for food security, and rehabilitation programs.
