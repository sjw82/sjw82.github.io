Hailey B. Mothershead STEAM Magnet High School

Stakeholders

In our efforts to draw on the many communities from which our students come, we have recently expanded our collaboration with stakeholders. We hope to encourage community members to feel a greater sense of collective ownership through several new initiatives including parent workshops, after school briefings, and community involvement.

We offer workshops to help parents and guardians support their student’s academic success; information regarding students’ options after graduation, including help with college applications; and have partnered with Literacy Pittsburgh to provide services to guardians of students who would like access to adult literacy or English as a second language tutoring. In addition to in person workshops, guardians can access resources on our Resources for Guardians page for information for themselves and their students.

H.B.M. also invites students’ guardians and other stakeholders to join us at bimonthly meetings to check in on the school’s status regarding many criteria, including current curriculum, budgets, student achievement, upcoming testing, and any possible changes the school might undergo; our aim is to provide as much transparency as possible. We also leave space for guardians and community members to provide feedback during the meeting. In order to make these meetings as accessible as possible, we broadcast them via a livefeed on this website and viewers can post messages to have their comments heard and questions answered.

Our third and most pervasive effort to include stakeholders have been several new programs within the schools, namely the LGBTQ+ alliance in partnership with PGH Equality Center, the sensory friendly study wing in partnership with UPMC, the renewed performing and visual arts center in partnership with the Pittsburgh Cultural Trust, and several advancements in our science and technology labs in partnership with Google.
